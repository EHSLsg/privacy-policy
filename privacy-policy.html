2. Information We Collect

2.1 Location Information

GPS Location Data (Precise Location)

When you enable location services, the App collects your precise GPS coordinates to:

  • Determine your current location and route status
  • Detect when you are on or near the Tuas Second Link or Woodlands Causeway routes (within 2 kilometers)
  • Activate location-based Bluetooth scanning when you are on supported routes
  • Display your current position on the map
  • Provide accurate route-specific traffic information
Key Points:
  • Location data is processed locally on your device for geofence detection
  • GPS coordinates are not stored permanently on our servers
  • Location data is used only for route detection and is not shared with third parties
  • You can disable location services at any time in your device settings

Multi-Zone Geofencing System

The App uses an advanced polygon-based geofencing system to detect your location across 5 distinct zones spanning from Singapore to Penang, Malaysia:

Zone Name Coverage Area Distance from SG Routes Shown
Singapore Zone Latitude 1.20-1.45°N 0-50km SG→MY only
Border Zone Latitude 1.45-1.46°N (2km buffer) ~50km All 4 routes
Johor Zone Latitude 1.46-2.5°N 50-200km MY→SG only
Central Malaysia (KL) Latitude 2.5-4.0°N 200-400km MY→SG only
Northern Malaysia (Penang) Latitude 4.0-5.5°N 400km+ MY→SG only

How It Works: The geofencing system uses a point-in-polygon algorithm with Haversine distance calculations to determine your location. This system is completely offline and does not require any API calls to detect your zone. The zone detection enables smart route filtering, which reduces API calls by 50-60% by only fetching traffic data for routes relevant to your location.

Coarse Location (Approximate Location)

On Android devices, we may collect coarse location information (approximate location based on cell towers and WiFi networks) as a fallback when precise GPS is unavailable. This helps us estimate your location when GPS signal is weak.

2.2 Traffic Data Collection

Google Maps Routes API

The App fetches real-time traffic data from Google Maps Routes API to provide accurate drive time estimates. Specifically, we collect:

  • Drive time with live traffic (duration_in_traffic)
  • Drive time without traffic (static_duration)
  • Route distance
  • Traffic congestion level

Data Retention: Traffic data is cached for 8 minutes to reduce API costs and improve performance. After 8 minutes, the data is automatically discarded and refreshed.

Smart Refresh Logic: The App only fetches traffic data for routes relevant to your current location:

  • If in Singapore: fetch SG→MY routes only (2 routes instead of 4)
  • If in Malaysia: fetch MY→SG routes only (2 routes instead of 4)
  • If near border: fetch all 4 routes (user might be crossing)

This smart filtering reduces API calls by 50-60% compared to fetching all 4 routes every time.

LTA DataMall API (Camera Feeds)

The App fetches live traffic camera images from Singapore's Land Transport Authority (LTA) to provide visual confirmation of traffic conditions. We collect:

  • Live camera images from Tuas Second Link checkpoint
  • Live camera images from Woodlands Causeway checkpoint
  • Camera metadata (timestamp, location)

Data Retention: Camera images are refreshed every 20 seconds and are not permanently stored on our servers.

Currency Exchange API

The App fetches live SGD to MYR currency exchange rates to help users plan their trips. We collect:

  • Current exchange rates (SGD to MYR and vice versa)
  • Historical exchange rate data (for chart display)
  • Timestamp of rate updates

Data Retention: Exchange rates are cached and refreshed periodically. Historical data is retained for chart visualization purposes.

2.3 Bluetooth Device Data

Nearby Device Detection (Bluetooth Low Energy Scanning)

When you enable Bluetooth scanning in the App settings, the App scans for nearby Bluetooth devices (phones, vehicles, IoT devices, wearables) to estimate traffic congestion based on device density. Specifically, we collect:

  • Number of nearby Bluetooth devices detected (e.g., 15 devices)
  • Received Signal Strength Indicator (RSSI) values to estimate distance from your device
  • Aggregated device density at each checkpoint (Tuas and Woodlands)
  • Timestamp of when the scan occurred
  • Checkpoint location (Tuas or Woodlands only)
What We Do NOT Collect:
  • ✗ Device names or Bluetooth identifiers
  • ✗ Personal information from nearby devices
  • ✗ MAC addresses or other unique device identifiers
  • ✗ Any data that could identify individuals
  • ✗ Information about specific devices or their owners

Geofence-Based Activation

The Bluetooth scanning operates on a geofence-based system to minimize battery drain and ensure data collection is relevant:

  • Bluetooth scanning is automatically activated when you enter the 2km border zone
  • Scanning is automatically deactivated when you leave the border zone
  • You can manually enable/disable Bluetooth scanning in App settings
  • Bluetooth scanning is completely optional and not required to use the App

2.4 App Usage Data

We collect limited usage data to improve the App:

  • App crash reports (to fix bugs)
  • Feature usage statistics (which routes are viewed most)
  • Settings preferences (dark mode, refresh interval)
  • Custom location data (if you save custom locations)

Data Retention: Usage data is retained for 90 days for analysis and improvement purposes.
