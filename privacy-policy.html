1. Introduction

Welcome to SG-MY Traffic (the "App"), a mobile application designed to provide real-time traffic information, congestion monitoring, and route planning for travelers between Singapore and Malaysia. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard your information when you use our App and related services.

This Privacy Policy applies to users in Singapore, Malaysia, and other jurisdictions. We comply with:

  • Google Play Store Privacy Requirements
  • Singapore Personal Data Protection Act (PDPA) 2012
  • Malaysia Personal Data Protection Act 2010 (PDPA)
  • Apple App Store Guidelines

Please read this Privacy Policy carefully. If you do not agree with our policies and practices, please do not use our App. By accessing and using SG-MY Traffic, you acknowledge that you have read and understood this Privacy Policy.

2. Information We Collect

2.1 Location Information

GPS Location Data (Precise Location)

When you enable location services, the App collects your precise GPS coordinates to:

  • Determine your current location and route status
  • Detect when you are on or near the Tuas Second Link or Woodlands Causeway routes (within 2 kilometers)
  • Activate location-based Bluetooth scanning when you are on supported routes
  • Display your current position on the map
  • Provide accurate route-specific traffic information
Key Points:
  • Location data is processed locally on your device for geofence detection
  • GPS coordinates are not stored permanently on our servers
  • Location data is used only for route detection and is not shared with third parties
  • You can disable location services at any time in your device settings

Coarse Location (Approximate Location)

On Android devices, we may collect coarse location information (approximate location based on cell towers and WiFi networks) as a fallback when precise GPS is unavailable. This helps us estimate your location when GPS signal is weak.

2.2 Bluetooth Device Data

Nearby Device Detection (Bluetooth Low Energy Scanning)

When you enable Bluetooth scanning in the App settings, the App scans for nearby Bluetooth devices (phones, vehicles, IoT devices, wearables) to estimate traffic congestion based on device density. Specifically, we collect:

  • Number of nearby Bluetooth devices detected (e.g., 15 devices)
  • Received Signal Strength Indicator (RSSI) values to estimate distance from your device
  • Aggregated device density at each checkpoint (Tuas and Woodlands)
  • Timestamp of when the scan occurred
  • Checkpoint location (Tuas or Woodlands only)
What We Do NOT Collect:
  • ✗ Device names or Bluetooth identifiers
  • ✗ Personal information from nearby devices
  • ✗ MAC addresses or other unique device identifiers
  • ✗ Any data that could identify individuals
  • ✗ Information about specific devices or their owners

Geofence-Based Activation

The Bluetooth scanning operates on a geofence-based system to minimize battery drain and ensure data collection is relevant:

  • Scanning automatically activates when you are within 2 kilometers of Tuas or Woodlands checkpoints
  • Scanning automatically stops when you leave these areas
  • You can manually enable/disable Bluetooth scanning in Settings at any time
  • Scanning only occurs when the App is in the foreground or minimized (not when completely closed)

2.3 Traffic and Route Data

Route Information

We collect information about the routes you view and interact with, including:

  • Which routes you select (Tuas Second Link, Woodlands Causeway)
  • Direction of travel (Singapore to Malaysia or vice versa)
  • Time and frequency of your route queries
  • Duration of time spent viewing traffic information
  • Route details you view (cameras, forecasts, trends)

Traffic Duration

We receive real-time traffic duration data from Google Maps Routes API, which includes:

  • Estimated travel time with current traffic conditions
  • Static travel time without traffic
  • Distance of the route
  • Traffic status (Light, Moderate, Heavy)
  • Route geometry and waypoints

This data is aggregated and does not identify you personally.

2.4 Saved Locations and Preferences

Destinations and Favorites

If you use the Destinations feature to save favorite locations or routes, we store:

  • Home location (optional)
  • Work location (optional)
  • Custom saved locations
  • Favorite routes
  • Location names and addresses
Storage Method:
  • This data is stored locally on your device using encrypted local storage (AsyncStorage)
  • We do not transmit this data to our servers unless you explicitly opt-in to cloud synchronization features
  • This data is not shared with third parties

2.5 Camera Feed Data

Live Traffic Cameras

The App displays live traffic camera feeds from the Land Transport Authority (LTA) of Singapore. We collect:

  • Which camera feeds you view
  • Duration of viewing each camera
  • Frequency of camera access
  • Camera IDs accessed
Important:
  • Camera feed images are not stored by us
  • Camera images are provided by LTA and are displayed in real-time only
  • We only log your interaction with these feeds for analytics purposes
  • Camera data is not shared with third parties

2.10 Crowdsourced Traffic Data (Optional)

Aggregated Congestion Data

When you enable Bluetooth scanning, we collect and aggregate device density data to provide crowd-sourced congestion estimates. This includes:

  • Device count at each checkpoint
  • Timestamp of the data point
  • Checkpoint location
  • Aggregated congestion level (Light, Moderate, Heavy)
Important:
  • This data is anonymized and cannot identify you
  • Individual data points are deleted after 24-48 hours
  • Only aggregated statistics are retained long-term
  • This data is used only for traffic analysis and is not shared with third parties

  4. Comply with Laws: Fulfill legal obligations and respond to lawful requests from authorities
  5. Communicate Updates: Send you important information about App updates, changes to this Privacy Policy, or service announcements
  6. Traffic Analysis: Create traffic pattern reports and historical trend data

5.2 Server Storage

When you enable Bluetooth scanning, we collect and store aggregated device density data on our servers to provide crowd-sourced congestion estimates. This data includes:

  • Number of nearby devices detected

Important:

  • This data cannot be used to identify you

Encryption at Rest

  • Sensitive data stored on our servers is encrypted using AES-256 encryption
  • Database access is restricted to authorized personnel only
  • Encryption keys are securely managed

Access Controls

  • Only authorized personnel have access to sensitive data
  • Role-based access control (RBAC) is implemented
  • Access logs are maintained and monitored

Regular Security Audits

  • We conduct regular security assessments
  • Penetration testing is performed quarterly

  • Security patches are applied promptly

Secure Authentication

  • Secure authentication mechanisms for API access
